## Overview

Report on a summer internship focused on how intrinsically disordered proteins (IDPs) — α-synuclein, Aβ42, and τ — interact with neuronal model membranes, in the context of neurodegenerative disease.

Three experimental threads:

1. **MEV isolation and BBB crossing** — Mycobacterial extracellular vesicles isolated from *M. tuberculosis* H37Ra cultures, characterised by DLS and BCA assay, and detected in mouse brain tissue by immunostaining to assess blood–brain barrier penetration.

2. **Protein expression** — BL21 *E. coli* transformed with pESAT (ESAT-6) and PT-77 (α-synuclein) plasmids; induction confirmed by SDS-PAGE.

3. **LUV preparation for anisotropy studies** — Large unilamellar vesicles of defined lipid composition (DOPC, DOPS, BPC, BPS, TBE, and mixtures) prepared by extrusion and sonication for membrane anisotropy measurements with IDP candidates.
